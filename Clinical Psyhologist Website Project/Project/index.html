About Dr. Fariha Naz

Welcome! I’m Fariha Naz, a qualified and compassionate Clinical Psychologist with over three years of professional experience in the assessment, diagnosis, and treatment of a wide range of psychological and emotional concerns. My work focuses on helping individuals—both children and adults—navigate emotional, psychological, and behavioral challenges using evidence-based therapeutic approaches.

My clinical approach is grounded in evidence-based practices

I strive to create a therapeutic environment that is safe, collaborative, and focused on long-term emotional well-being. In addition to my clinical work, I have a strong academic background and have authored four peer-reviewed research articles in recognized psychology journals. My research interests include emotional regulation, therapeutic interventions, and mental health education. My professional philosophy is rooted in empathy, integrity, and a commitment to ongoing learning. I believe in empowering individuals to understand themselves, overcome challenges, and lead more meaningful and mentally healthy lives.

Mental health is not a destination, but a process. It's about how you drive, not where you're going.

ABA is a structured, evidence-based therapy that focuses on understanding and improving behaviors by applying principles of learning and motivation. It is especially effective for children with autism and developmental delays. ABA helps improve communication, social skills, attention, and daily living skills by reinforcing positive behaviors and reducing those that interfere with learning

Common questions about therapy and my practice

I work with children, adolescents, and adults dealing with a wide range of mental health concerns including anxiety, depression, trauma, OCD, anger, behavioral issues, ADHD, Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD), and all neurodevelopmental and psychological challenges.

The first session is an intake or assessment session where we discuss your concerns, history, and goals for therapy. It’s a chance for you to ask questions and for us to create a comfortable, supportive plan tailored to your needs.

Each individual session typically lasts 45–60 minutes. In some cases, initial assessments or child therapy sessions may vary slightly in duration depending on the need.

The number of sessions depends on your individual situation and progress. Some clients benefit from short-term therapy (6–12 sessions), while others continue longer for deeper emotional work or ongoing support.
